Stevie Nicks

Singer | Songwriter

Stevie Nicks of Fleetwood Mac performing live at The Oakland Coliseum, 1977, Oakland, CA (Photo by Richard McCaffrey/ Michael Ochs Archive/ Getty Images)

Timeline of Stevie Nicks:

  • 1948: Born Stephanie Nicks in Phoenix, Arizona
  • 1964: Received a Goya guitar for 16th birthday, and wrote her first song titled "I've Loved and I've Lost, and I'm Sad but Not Blue"
  • 1967: Met Lindsey Buckingham and was asked by Buckingham to replace lead singer of band "Fritz"
  • 1968: Lead singer for "Fritz" until 1970 and opened for Jimi Hendrix and Janis Joplin
  • 1972: Signed first record deal with Polydor Records
  • 1973: Released first EP titled Buckingham Nicks
    • Nicks wrote "Rhiannon" and "Landslide" after album was not successful and relationship was deteriorating with Buckingham
  • 1974: Mick Fleetwood heard the track "Frozen Love" from the Buckingham Nicks EP and invited Buckingham to join his band "Fleetwood Mac". Buckingham refused unless Nicks was invited as well
  • 1975: "Fleetwood Mac" released a self-titled album. "Rhiannon" and "Landslide" were huge hits on in the album
    • Nicks then worked with clothing designer Margi Kent to develop a unique onstage look
    • Nicks ended relationship with Buckingham
  • 1976: Recorded the album Rumours and sang backup on Warren Zevons's 2nd EP
    • One of Nicks' contributions to Rumors was "Dreams" and became the band's only Billboard Hot 100 number-one single
    • Nicks wrote and recorded the song "Silver Springs", which was written about her relationship with Buckingham. Buckingham wrote and recorded "Go Your Own Way" regarding their relationship as well.
    • Nicks and Fleetwood began an affair (Fleetwood was married to Jenny Boyd) and ended it by October of 1978
  • 1978: Rumours was the best-selling album of 1977 and won a Grammy for Album of the Year in 1978
    • Nicks recorded duet "Whenever I call you friend" with Kenny Loggins
  • 1979: "Fleetwood Mac" released album Tusk and toured through 1980
    • Nicks sang backup on most tracks of Not Shy recorded by Walter Egan
    • Nicks recorded song "Gold" with John Stewart
  • 1981: Nicks released first solo album Bella Donna, and four singles reached Billboard Hot 100
    • Nicks made appearances with "Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers"
    • Nicks married best friend Robin Anderson's widower, Kim Anderson, after she passed from lukemia. They divorced three years later due to grief.
  • 1983: Nicks released second solo album The Wild Heart and it went double platinum
    • Performed "Stand Back" and "Nightbird" on Saturday Night Live
  • 1985: Nicks released third solo album Rock a Little in November and toured with Bob Dylan and "Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers" in Austraila
    • After Joplin and Hendrix passed, Nicks saw a physician about her health and cocaine addiction and checked herself into the Betty Ford Center
  • 1987: "Fleetwood Mac" album Tango in the Night was released after Nicks recorded parts at the Betty Ford Center; this was the band's second-highest selling album.
    • Nicks suffered through chronic fatigue syndrome and developed an addiction to Klonopin after Buckingham quit the group in 1987, but Nicks resumed touring in 1988
  • 1989: Nicks released a fourth solo album titled The Other Side of the Mirror
  • 1990: "Fleetwood Mac" album Behind the Mask released and was certified platinum and number one in the U.K.
    • Nicks left group after disagreement with Mick Fleetwood to release track "Silver Springs" on her album Timespace: The Best of Stevie Nicks
  • 1991: Nicks released Timespace: The Best of Stevie Nicks. The following year, "Fleetwood Mac" released a four-disc box set 25 Years - The Chain
    • "Fleetwood Mac" performed "Don't Stop" at Bill Cinton's 1993 inaugural gala
  • 1994: Nicks released her fifth solo album Street Angel
  • 1996: Nicks and Buckingham reunited for the duet "Twisted" for the move Twister
    • Nicks also covered Tom Petty's "Free Fallin'" for TV show Party of Five
  • 1997: "Fleetwood Mac" reunited for tour The Dance
  • 1988: "Fleetwood Mac" was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and awarded the Outstanding Contribution at the BRIT Awards
  • 1998: Nicks contributed to the Practical Magic soundtrack and released the box set Enchanted
  • 2001: Sixth solo album Trouble in Shangri-La was released and song "Planets of the Universe was nominated for Best Female Rock vocal Performance
    • Nicks was named VH1's Artist of the Month and People magazine's 50 Most Beautiful People
  • 2003: "Fleetwood Mac" released album Say You Will and toured through 2004
  • 2005: Nicks performed at Caesars Palace in Las Vegas and toured with Vanessa Carlton for over 20 dates
  • 2007: Album Crystal Visions - The Very Best of Stevie Nicks and it debuted at 21 on the Billboard 200
  • 2009: Nicks released live album The Soundstage Sessions
    • "Fleetwood Mac" performed world tour Unleashed
  • 2010: Nicks performed a seried on shows in Santa Barbara to benefit a young girl with rhabdomyosarcoma, whom she met through the Make-a-Wish Foundation
  • 2011: Nicks' seventh solo album In Your Dreams and debuted at number six on the Billboard 200
  • 2012: Dave Grohl collaborated with Nicks and other artists on his Sound City soundtrack
  • 2014: "Fleetwood Mac" toured throughout North America and Europe, then released a four-track EP titled Extended Play
  • 2014: Nicks' eighth solo album 24 Karat Gold: Songs from the Vault was released
    • Nicks appeared on the third season of American Horror Story: Coven and eighth season American Horro Story: Apocalypse
  • 2016: Nicks toured with "The Pretenders" on the 24 Karat Gold Tour
  • 2017: Nicks was featured on track "Beautiful People Beautiful Problems" with Lana Del Rey
    • Before Tom Petty passed, Nicks performed in London with "Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers"
  • 2019: Nicks was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, making her the first woman to be inducted twice
  • 2020: Nicks released new song "Show Them the Way"
    • Nicks' song catalog was sold at an 80% stake to publishing company Primary Wave valued at $100 million
  • 2021: Nicks was announced as one of the Headliners of the 2021 Shaky Knees Music Festival in Atlanta, Georgia
